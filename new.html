Case Study
Leading Implementations: Lessons Learned
Mayo Clinic: AI-Assisted ECG Analysis
  • Detected asymptomatic left ventricular dysfunction via standard ECG with 93% AUC — invisible to human readers
  • Early detection led to treatment before heart failure onset in 34% of flagged patients
  • Deployed at scale across 1.2M patients; now integrated into routine care pathways
  • Key learning: Clinical champion buy-in and workflow integration are more critical than algorithm performance
NHS UK: AI Breast Cancer Screening
  • Google Health AI matched or exceeded radiologist accuracy in breast cancer detection across 29,000 mammograms
  • Reduced false positives by 5.7% and false negatives by 9.4% vs. standard double reading
  • Currently in expanded NHS England pilot across 30 sites — results expected Q3 2025
  • Key learning: Diverse training data is essential; AI trained on single demographics shows 12% accuracy drop in other groups
IBM Watson for Oncology: Cautionary Lessons
  • Watson for Oncology recommended "unsafe and incorrect" treatments in 5 of 7 cancer types per internal Sloan Kettering audit
  • Training data was too limited, non-diverse & derived from synthetic cases rather than real patient outcomes
  • Project discontinued in 2022 — highlights risks of over-promising AI in high-stakes clinical settings
  • Key learning: Rigorous real-world validation must precede clinical deployment — no exceptions
Insilico Medicine: AI Drug Discovery
  • Identified novel fibrosis drug target & designed candidate molecule in 18 months vs. typical 4–5 years
  • INS018_055 passed Phase I safety trials in 2023 and entered Phase II in 2024 for idiopathic pulmonary fibrosis
  • Total cost to Phase II: ~$2.6M vs. industry average of $300M+ for traditional discovery
  • Key learning: AI excels at hypothesis generation; human expertise remains essential for mechanistic interpretation

Strategic Outlook
Risks, Ethics & adelphi Recommendations
Critical Risk Areas
  • Algorithmic Bias & Health EquityAI trained on non-diverse datasets systematically underperforms for minority, elderly & low-income populations
  • Regulatory LagFDA & EMA frameworks still lag the pace of AI capability development — creating compliance uncertainty
  • Data Privacy & SecurityHealthcare data breaches rose 93% in 2024; AI systems expand the attack surface considerably
  • Clinician Automation BiasOver-reliance on AI recommendations can erode clinical judgment — particularly among trainees
  • Liability & Accountability GapsWho is responsible when AI-assisted diagnosis leads to patient harm? Legal frameworks remain unresolved
adelphi Strategic Recommendations
  • Establish an AI Clinical Governance BoardCross-functional oversight including clinicians, ethicists, data scientists & patient representatives
  • Mandate Prospective Clinical ValidationAll AI tools must demonstrate real-world performance across diverse patient populations before deployment
  • Invest in AI Literacy for Clinical StaffTarget 100% of clinical staff completing AI fundamentals training by end of 2025
  • Adopt an "AI Augments, Clinician Decides" PolicyPreserve human decision authority in all diagnostic, treatment & triage workflows
  • Prioritise Interoperability & Open StandardsFHIR-compliant, vendor-neutral architectures prevent costly lock-in and enable data portability
